Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in the city of Agra, India. It was commissioned by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it is considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and a symbol of eternal love.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi, India. It is a 73-meter tall minaret and the tallest brick minaret in the world. Built in the early 13th century, it is a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital city of the Indian state of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City due to the distinctive color of its buildings. It is famous for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture. Popular attractions in Jaipur include the City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amer Fort.

Goa

Goa is a coastal state in western India known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. It offers a unique blend of sun, sand, sea, and spirituality. Popular attractions in Goa include Calangute Beach, Baga Beach, Basilica of Bom Jesus, and Fort Aguada.

Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala Backwaters are a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons in the state of Kerala, India. It is a popular tourist destination known for its serene beauty, lush greenery, and traditional houseboat cruises.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a prominent Sikh gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is one of the most revered spiritual sites in Sikhism and attracts millions of visitors from all over the world every year.

Mumbai

Mumbai, formerly known as Bombay, is the financial and entertainment capital of India. It is a bustling metropolis known for its iconic landmarks, diverse culture, and vibrant lifestyle. Popular attractions in Mumbai include the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and Elephanta Caves.

Ladakh

Ladakh is a region in the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its breathtaking landscapes, rugged mountains, and Buddhist monasteries. It offers unique experiences such as trekking, camping, and exploring ancient Tibetan Buddhist culture.

Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. These rock-cut caves represent a fascinating blend of Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist religious art and architecture dating back to the 6th century CE.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace located in the city of Mysore, Karnataka, India. It is one of the most magnificent royal residences in India, known for its Indo-Saracenic architecture, intricate artwork, and grandeur.
